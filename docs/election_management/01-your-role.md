# Your Role as an Election Officer

## What Is an Election Officer?

When your organisation runs a real election, the organisation administrator appoints a team of **Election Officers** to oversee it. There are three roles:

---

## The Three Officer Roles

### 👑 Chief Election Officer

The highest authority for the election. Responsible for the full lifecycle:

- Opens and closes the voting period
- Approves and suspends voters on the voter list
- Publishes the election results when voting ends
- Has full access to the Management Dashboard

> **Only one chief is typically appointed per election.**

---

### 🤝 Deputy Election Officer

Supports the Chief. Has the same day-to-day powers *except* publishing results:

- Can open and close voting
- Can approve and suspend voters
- Cannot publish or unpublish results
- Has full access to the Management Dashboard

> **Deputies are useful for shared responsibility or backup when the chief is unavailable.**

---

### 👁️ Commissioner

An observer role. Can monitor the election but cannot change anything:

- Views the Viewboard (live election status and statistics)
- Cannot approve voters, open voting, or publish results
- Receives the same invitation email and must accept to gain access

> **Commissioners are typically neutral party observers who verify the process is fair.**

---

## Role Comparison

```
                        Chief    Deputy   Commissioner
                        ─────    ──────   ────────────
View election status      ✓        ✓          ✓
View voter list           ✓        ✓          ✓
Approve a voter           ✓        ✓          ✗
Suspend a voter           ✓        ✓          ✗
Open voting               ✓        ✓          ✗

Publish results           ✓        ✗          ✗
Unpublish results         ✓        ✗          ✗
Management Dashboard      ✓        ✓          ✗
Viewboard                 ✓        ✓          ✓
```

---

## Status: Pending vs Active

When you are first appointed, your status is **Pending**. You **cannot access any election pages** until you accept your appointment.

After clicking "Accept Appointment" in your invitation email, your status becomes **Active** and you can log in and use the system.

```
Appointed by admin
       ↓
  Status: PENDING
  (no access yet)
       ↓
  You click email link and accept
       ↓
  Status: ACTIVE
  (full role access)
```

> **Important:** Your invitation link expires in 7 days. If it expires, ask your organisation administrator to re-appoint you.

---

## What Happens If I Was Previously an Officer?

If you were an officer for a previous election and are reappointed:
- Your previous record is restored
- You receive a fresh invitation email
- You must accept again to become active
