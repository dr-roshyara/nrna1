# Reviewing Contributions: Admin Guide

This guide walks admins through the contribution review workflow, from submission to approval.

---

## The Review Workflow

```
Pending       Review      Verify      Approve/Reject    Complete
  (1)           (2)         (3)           (4)              (5)
   ↓             ↓           ↓             ↓                ↓
Submitted    Check details  Check proof   Award points   Member notified
             & description  & evidence    or ask Qs      Points visible
```

---

## Step 1: Identify Pending Contributions

**Where to find them:**
- Admin dashboard → Contributions → "Pending" status filter
- Or: Notifications → New contributions awaiting review

**Information visible:**
- Member name
- Contribution title & description
- Track (Micro / Standard / Major)
- Hours claimed
- Submission date
- Skills claimed
- Proof type

**Sort by:**
- Oldest first (review in order)
- Track (review Micro bulk, then Standard, then Major)
- Member (group by same person)

---

## Step 2: Review the Contribution

Open the contribution detail page. You'll see:

### A. Member & Basic Info
- **Contributor**: Who submitted it?
- **Date submitted**: How recent?
- **Track**: Micro / Standard / Major
- **Status**: Currently "Pending"

### B. Contribution Details
- **Title**: One-sentence summary
- **Description**: Full details of what was done
- **Hours**: Effort claimed
- **Skills**: What skills were involved

**Review checklist:**
- ✓ Description is clear and specific
- ✓ Hours seem reasonable
- ✓ Skills listed match the contribution type
- ✓ No red flags (implausible claims, etc.)

### C. Points Preview
System shows estimated points:
```
Base: 8 hrs × 10 = 80 pts
+ 50 pts (Standard tier)
× 1.2 (2 skills)
× 0.7 (photo proof)
× 1.0 (one-time)
= 84 points (estimated)
```

This is what they'll earn **if approved**.

---

## Step 3: Verify the Proof

### For Micro Contributions
- ✓ Just read the description
- ✓ Does it sound reasonable?
- ✓ Does the member have a history of honest reporting?
- No proof upload required

**Decision**: Usually Approve (unless something seems off)

### For Standard Contributions
- Check the **proof file** uploaded
- Proof types: Photo, Document, Third-party email

**For Photos**:
- ✓ Shows the activity or end result
- ✓ Matches the description
- ✓ Looks recent (not obviously old)
- Accept or ask member for more photos

**For Documents**:
- ✓ Email, letter, or written confirmation
- ✓ Mentions the member's name and activity
- ✓ Comes from a credible source
- Accept or ask for clarification

**For Third-Party Vouchers**:
- ✓ Written by someone other than member
- ✓ Confirms the member's participation
- ✓ Ideally from someone known to org
- Accept or ask member for different reference

**Decision**: Approve (if proof matches), Request clarification, or Reject (if no proof)

### For Major Contributions
- Check the **institutional proof**
- Types: Official letter, contract, partnership doc, certificate

**Verification steps**:
1. ✓ Letter is from a legitimate organization (school, NGO, hospital, etc.)
2. ✓ Letter specifically names the contributor
3. ✓ Letter describes the work and outcome
4. ✓ Letter is signed and dated
5. ✓ Letter mentions measurable impact (# of people served, etc.)

**If letter seems unofficial**:
- Email the organization (if address is on letterhead)
- Ask: "Can you confirm that [person] did this work?"
- Wait for reply before approving

**Decision**: Approve (if verified), Request clarification, or Reject (if unverifiable)

---

## Step 4: Make a Decision

### Option A: Approve

Click **Approve** if:
- ✓ Description is clear and credible
- ✓ Hours seem reasonable
- ✓ Proof matches or is not required
- ✓ Member seems trustworthy

**When you approve:**
1. The system calculates final points using the formula
2. Points are **instantly awarded**
3. An immutable ledger entry is created
4. Member gets a notification
5. Leaderboard updates (if they're visible)

### Option B: Request Clarification

Click **Ask for More Info** if:
- ? Hours seem unclear ("was it really 40 hours?")
- ? Proof doesn't clearly match description
- ? Skills listed don't match the work
- ? Need more details on outcome

**Write a note** like:
> "Hi [Member], thanks for logging this! Can you clarify—the description says you taught 4 weeks, but you logged 6 hours total. That's 1.5 hours per week. Is that right? Or did you mean 4 hours per week?"

**Member gets notified** and can edit/resubmit.

### Option C: Reject

Click **Reject** if:
- ❌ Description is vague or implausible
- ❌ No proof despite being required
- ❌ Proof clearly doesn't match
- ❌ Hours are unrealistic
- ❌ Member has history of misreporting
- ❌ Activity isn't appropriate for org

**Write a note** explaining why:
> "Hi [Member], thanks for submitting. Unfortunately, the photo you uploaded doesn't match your description of 'teaching a class.' Can you provide evidence that this activity actually happened? Or feel free to log a different contribution."

**Member gets notified** and can:
- Appeal the rejection
- Log a different contribution
- Request help from an admin

---

## Step 5: Notify Member

After you approve/reject/ask for info:

- ✓ Member gets an **automatic notification**
- ✓ They can see your feedback
- ✓ They can edit (if asking for clarification)
- ✓ They can appeal (if rejected)

**Write friendly, constructive notes.** Remember: these are volunteers! A gentle tone matters.

## Managing the Outcome Bonus

For **major contributions** or **exceptional work**, you can add an **Outcome Bonus**: an extra 0–200 points if the impact was significant.

### When to Award Outcome Bonus

**Good reasons:**
- ✓ Contribution impacted 100+ people
- ✓ Community-wide initiative
- ✓ Measurable, lasting change
- ✓ High-impact project

**Not good reasons:**
- ❌ "I liked this person"
- ❌ "They've been here a long time"
- ❌ Purely subjective impact

### How Much to Award

| Impact Level | Bonus |
|---|---|
| Good outcome (small group helped) | +25–50 |
| Strong outcome (50+ people) | +50–100 |
| Excellent outcome (100+ people) | +100–150 |
| Transformational outcome | +150–200 |

### Example

**Member**: Dr. Patel  
**Contribution**: Ran scholarship fund (60 hours, institutional proof)  
**Base calculation**: 1,440 points  
**Outcome**: Funded 25 scholarships, changed lives  
**Decision**: Add +100 outcome bonus  
**Final**: 1,540 points

## Bulk Review Tips

### Reviewing Multiple Contributions

1. **Sort by date** — Review oldest first (keep queue moving)
2. **Group by type** — Do all Micro, then Standard, then Major
3. **Batch similar ones** — Approve 5 Micro quickly, then tackle harder reviews
4. **Take breaks** — Reviewing is tiring; don't do it all at once

### Common Patterns to Watch

- **Inflated hours**: Micro contributions claiming 8 hours (should be max 3–5)
- **Vague descriptions**: "Helped with stuff" — ask for details
- **No proof**: Standard track claiming "no pictures" — require proof or downgrade to Micro
- **Unrealistic outcomes**: "Taught 100 people in 4 hours" — unlikely

### Quality Gate

**Approve rate**: Aim for ~80–90% approval  
- If approving 100%, you're being too lenient
- If rejecting 50%+, you're being too strict

## Appeal Process

If member disagrees with a rejection:

1. **Member clicks "Appeal"** on their contribution
2. **You get a notification** that it's appealed
3. **Member explains** why they disagree
4. **You review** their explanation
5. **You can**:
   - Accept the appeal → Approve
   - Maintain the rejection → Closed
   - Request more evidence → Back to member

**Keep appeals brief.** If it's contentious, talk to the member directly.

## The Points Ledger

Every approval creates an **immutable ledger entry**:

```
Ledger Entry #423
User: Dr. Patel
Contribution: Scholarship fund
Points: +1,540
Action: earned
Reason: "Track: major, Proof: institutional, Outcome bonus: +100"
Date: 2026-04-15
```

**Ledger is permanent:**
- ✓ Can't edit (only admins can add correction rows)
- ✓ Shows full calculation
- ✓ Auditable forever

**Message**: "Hi Dr. Patel, this looks like significant work! For major track, I need official documentation. Can you get a letter from the school/organization confirming the scholarship program and your role?"  
**Follow-up**: She gets a letter → Approve with institutional proof

## Resolving Disputes

### If Member Disagrees with Your Decision

**Best practice:**
1. Respond politely and quickly
2. Explain your reasoning
3. Offer to clarify the rules
4. Don't take it personally
5. Escalate to senior admin if needed

**Sample response:**
> "Hi [Member], I appreciate you reaching out! I wanted to clarify my decision. For standard track, we require some kind of proof (photo, document, or third-party confirmation). You submitted self-report only. This means your points would be calculated at 0.5× multiplier, giving ~30 points instead of 60. Would you like to add proof and resubmit?"

## Regular Maintenance

### Weekly
- Check pending queue
- Approve/reject/clarify
- Send notifications

### Monthly
- Review any appeals
- Check for patterns (spam, gaming, etc.)
- Celebrate approved contributors

### Quarterly
- Audit random ledger entries for correctness
- Check for formula drift
- Update guidelines if needed

---

## Questions or Conflicts?

- **"Should I approve someone I don't trust?"** — Yes, if the proof is solid. Trust the system, not feelings.
- **"What if the proof is obviously fake?"** — Reject it. Admin can investigate if needed.
- **"How long should each review take?"** — Micro: 1 min, Standard: 5 min, Major: 15 min
- **"Can I make mistakes?"** — Yes, which is why the ledger is immutable. You can add corrections.
