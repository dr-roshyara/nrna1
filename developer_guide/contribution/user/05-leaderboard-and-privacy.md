# Leaderboard and Privacy

This guide explains the leaderboard, how it works, and how to control whether your name appears on it.

---

## What is the Leaderboard?

The **leaderboard** is a public ranking of your organization's most active contributors. It shows:

- ✓ Who contributed the most (by points earned)
- ✓ How many points each person has
- ✓ Rankings (1st, 2nd, 3rd place, etc.)
- ✓ A visual chart showing relative contribution

It's a **way to celebrate** your community's most active members.

---

## Why a Leaderboard?

### For Members
- Recognition of your hard work
- Motivation to stay engaged
- Public thanks from your community

### For Organizations
- Celebrating volunteer effort
- Identifying core contributors
- Building a culture of service
- Recognition ceremonies

---

## Three Privacy Options

When you set up your profile, you choose **who can see your name** on the leaderboard:

### Option 1: Public 🌐
**Your name appears with your full points.**

- ✓ "Dr. Sharma — 500 points"
- ✓ Everyone in your org can see it
- ✓ Best for: People who want recognition
- ✓ Good for: Building personal reputation

**Choose Public if:**
- You want to be recognized for your work
- You're comfortable with visibility
- You want to inspire others

### Option 2: Anonymous 🕵️
**You appear on the leaderboard, but as "Contributor #3" instead of your name.**

- ✓ "Contributor #5 — 500 points"
- ✓ Your rank and points are visible
- ✓ But your name is hidden
- ✓ Best for: Humble contributors or privacy-conscious members

**Choose Anonymous if:**
- You want recognition without personal publicity
- You prefer privacy but still want to participate
- Your family/work situation benefits from discretion

### Option 3: Private 🔒
**You don't appear on the leaderboard at all.**

- ✓ Your points are still counted
- ✓ Admins can still see your full history
- ✓ But the public leaderboard shows nothing
- ✓ Best for: People who prefer zero visibility

**Choose Private if:**
- You contribute for community benefit, not recognition
- You value complete privacy
- You want your work noted but not publicized

---

## How to Change Your Privacy Setting

1. Go to your **Profile**
2. Find **Leaderboard Visibility** or **Contribution Settings**
3. Choose: Public / Anonymous / Private
4. Save

You can change this **anytime**. It takes effect **immediately**.

## Understanding the Tiers

Your total points earn you badges:

| Tier | Points | Badge |
|---|---|---|
| **Bronze** | 50–149 | 🥉 |
| **Silver** | 150–299 | 🥈 |
| **Gold** | 300+ | 🏆 |

**Badges show recognition level:**
- 🥉 Bronze = Active contributor
- 🥈 Silver = Very active
- 🏆 Gold = Most active / core volunteer

## Can I Opt Out Completely?

**Yes!** You have three ways:

1. **Choose Private** — You contribute but don't appear on the leaderboard
2. **Don't log contributions** — If you don't log anything, you won't be on the leaderboard
3. **Leave the organization** — Your leaderboard appearance ends

**But remember:** The contribution system benefits your org. Even if you choose Private, your work is still recorded and valued. The leaderboard is just one part.

## Privacy and Data

**Your data is safe:**
- ✓ Only your organization can see the leaderboard
- ✓ Leaderboard is not public to the internet
- ✓ Your contact info is never shown
- ✓ Changing privacy settings doesn't change your contribution history

**Admin access:**
- ✓ Admins can see full details (name, proof, ledger)
- ✓ This is needed to verify contributions
- ✓ But they follow confidentiality protocols
- ✓ Your org should have a privacy policy about this

## Common Questions

### "If I choose Anonymous, can admins still see my contributions?"
Yes. Admins need to see full details to verify contributions. But the **public leaderboard** only shows "Contributor #5" — not your name.

### "Can I change my privacy setting after contributions are approved?"
Yes! You can change anytime, and it affects how you appear on the leaderboard **from that point forward**. Previous contributions are already recorded; you can't delete them.

### "What if I change from Public to Anonymous later?"
- ✓ Your name is immediately removed from the leaderboard
- ✓ A new "Contributor #X" number is assigned
- ✓ Your old contributions are still valid
- ✓ No points are lost, just the display name changes

### "If I'm Anonymous, can people guess who I am?"
Possible, but unlikely. "Contributor #5 — 500 points" doesn't reveal identity by itself. The number is just for display, not tied to your name.

### "Can I be listed as Private for some contributions and Public for others?"
No, it's an all-or-nothing setting. You're either Public, Anonymous, or Private across **all** your contributions.

### "What about family members? Can they see my points?"
**If they're in your organization**: Yes, they see the leaderboard like anyone else.
**If they're not**: No, unless they're admins.

---

## Recommendation

We recommend:
- **Be Public** if you're comfortable and want recognition
- **Be Anonymous** if you want recognition but privacy
- **Be Private** only if you truly don't want any visibility

Contributing itself is the most important thing. The leaderboard is just a bonus reward system.
