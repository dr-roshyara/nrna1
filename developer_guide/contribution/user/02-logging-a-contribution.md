# Logging a Contribution: Step-by-Step Guide

This guide walks you through filling out the contribution form and submitting your work for review.

---

## The Five Steps

When you log a contribution, you'll fill in:

1. **Contribution type** (Micro, Standard, or Major)
2. **Hours and description**
3. **Skills involved** (optional but rewarded)
4. **Proof of work** (optional but increases points)
5. **Is it recurring?** (for bonuses)

Then the system shows you **how many points you'll earn**, and you submit.

---

## Step 1: Pick the Track

When you click **"+ Log Contribution"**, you'll see three options:

### ⚡ Micro Track
- ✓ For quick, informal work
- ✓ No proof needed
- ✓ Self-reported (we trust you!)
- ✓ Max 100 points per week
- 📌 **Best for**: Teaching a child, mentoring a friend, helping a neighbor

### 📌 Standard Track
- ✓ For organized, verified work
- ✓ Light proof (photos, docs, third-party)
- ✓ 31–200 points
- 📌 **Best for**: Running a class, organizing an event, documented project

### 🏆 Major Track
- ✓ For significant projects
- ✓ Strong proof (official letter, institutional document)
- ✓ 201+ points
- 📌 **Best for**: Building something, major partnership, transformational work

**Choose the track that matches your effort level.** The system will guide you from there.

---

## Step 2: Add Hours and Description

### Hours Slider
Use the slider to select how many hours you spent:
- **Micro**: Usually 0.5–3 hours
- **Standard**: Usually 4–20 hours
- **Major**: Usually 30–100+ hours

As you slide, you'll see:
- How many points the base effort is worth (hours × 10)
- Progress toward the next "tier" (more on tiers below)

### Description
Fill in the **Title** and **Description**:

**Title** (one sentence):
- ✓ "Taught my grandchildren Nepali language"
- ✓ "Organized Teej cultural festival"
- ✓ "Ran weekly English conversation class"

**Description** (details):
- What did you do?
- Who did you help?
- What was the outcome?
- How long did it take?

**Example:**
> **Title**: Organizing Teej Cultural Celebration
> 
> **Description**: Led a team to organize and execute the annual Teej celebration for our community. Coordinated with 5 volunteers, arranged music, food, and decorations. Event had 150+ attendees and raised awareness of cultural traditions among younger generation members.

---

## Step 3: Select Skills (Optional But Valuable!)

When you select skills, you unlock the **synergy bonus**: teams with diverse skills earn up to **1.5× multiplier**.

**Available skills:**
- Teaching / Mentoring
- Healthcare / Counseling
- Engineering / Technical
- Legal / Administration
- Finance / Accounting
- Marketing / Communications
- Translation / Interpretation
- Event Planning / Organization

**How synergy works:**

| Skills Selected | Bonus |
|---|---|
| 1 skill (all the same) | 1.0× |
| 2 different skills | **1.2×** |
| 3+ different skills | **1.5×** |

### Example
Raj organized the Teej festival. He had:
- **Event Planning** (organized logistics)
- **Marketing** (promoted to the community)
- **Translation** (created materials in multiple languages)

= **3 different skills = 1.5× synergy bonus!**

This multiplier applies to all his points. It rewards **people who combine talents.**

---

## Step 4: Upload Proof (Optional But Rewarded!)

Your proof quality directly multiplies your points. Here are the options:

| Proof Type | Multiplier | Effort | Examples |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Self-Report** | 0.5× | None | Your word alone |
| **Photo** | 0.7× | Take a photo | Event photos, class photos, completed work |
| **Document** | 0.8× | Written proof | Letter, testimonial, written feedback |
| **Third-Party** | 1.0× | Someone vouches | Teacher's note, manager's email, peer confirmation |
| **Institutional** | 1.2× | Official document | Partnership letter, official certificate, contract |

### How to Use Proof

- **Self-Report** (0.5×): Just your description. No upload needed. Best for micro track.
- **Photo** (0.7×): Take a photo during or after your activity. Upload from your phone.
- **Document** (0.8×): Email from an organizer, feedback form, screenshot of completion.
- **Third-Party** (1.0×): Get someone else to write "I can confirm X did this work."
- **Institutional** (1.2×): Official letter from an organization (school, hospital, NGO).

### Example: English Class

**Without proof** (self-report):
- 4 hours × 10 = 40 base points
- 0.5× verification = **20 points**

**With photo proof**:
- 4 hours × 10 = 40 base points
- 0.7× verification = **28 points** (+8 bonus!)

**With third-party voucher** (students write confirmation):
- 4 hours × 10 = 40 base points
- 1.0× verification = **40 points** (+20 bonus!)

---

## Step 5: Recurring Activity?

Check this box if you do this work **regularly** (weekly, monthly, etc.):

- ✓ Weekly mentoring session
- ✓ Monthly language class
- ✓ Bi-weekly translation work
- ✓ Ongoing community support

**Bonus**: Recurring activities earn a **1.2× sustainability multiplier**. This rewards people who build lasting systems, not just one-off events.

### Example: Library Project

**One-time building event**:
- 40 hours × 10 = 400 base points
- No sustainability bonus = 1.0×
- **400 points total**

**Ongoing library maintenance** (weekly):
- 40 hours × 10 = 400 base points
- 1.2× sustainability bonus
- **480 points total** (+80 bonus!)

---

## The Final Step: Submit or Save Draft

When you're happy with your form:

- **Submit Contribution** — Send it for review. Status becomes "Pending."
- **Save Draft** — Save it privately. You can edit and submit later.

After submit, your contribution enters the review queue. **Usually within 3–5 days, an admin will:**

1. Read your description
2. Check your proof
3. Approve it (points awarded!) or ask questions

## Troubleshooting

### "My form won't submit"
- Did you fill in **Title** and **Description**? Both are required.
- Is your **Hours** number valid? (Should be 0.5–100+)
- Are you on an **Internet connection**? Check your browser's network.

### "How long until I see my points?"
- Your org leaders review contributions regularly, usually **3–5 days**.
- You'll see a notification when it's approved.

### "Can I edit after submitting?"
- Not directly. But you can message your org leader and ask for an amendment.
- They can mark it as "Appealed" and you can resubmit with corrections.

### "What if I made a mistake?"
- Don't worry! Message your org leader explaining the error.
- They can create a correction (negative adjustment) in the ledger.
- Then you can log a corrected version.
