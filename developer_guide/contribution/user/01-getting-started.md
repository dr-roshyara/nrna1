# Getting Started with Contributions

Welcome! This guide explains what contributions are, why they matter, and how to start earning recognition for your work.

---

## What is a Contribution?

A **contribution** is any work you do for your organisation — whether it's volunteering, mentoring, organizing an event, or sharing your skills. Instead of work going unrecognized, the contribution system records it and awards you points.

Examples from your community:

- **Aama** teaches her grandchildren Nepali language every week
- **Raj** organises a successful Teej cultural festival
- **Dr. Patel** runs a free weekly English conversation class for seniors
- **Meena** translates government documents for refugee families
- **Ahmed** leads a team building a community library

All of these are contributions.

---

## Why Does It Matter?

### For You
- **Visible recognition** — Your work is recorded permanently
- **Public credit** (if you choose) — Your name can appear on the leaderboard
- **Points system** — You earn points that reflect the impact of your work
- **Community status** — Active contributors become recognised community leaders

### For Your Organisation
- **Measurable impact** — Leaders understand where volunteer effort goes
- **Fair rewards** — The system rewards bigger efforts and quality proof
- **Team insights** — Members with complementary skills get extra credit
- **Transparent process** — Everyone can see how points are calculated

---

## Three Types of Contributions

The system recognises three tracks, each with different verification needs:

| Track | Size | Effort | Proof Required | Weekly Cap |
|-------|------|--------|---|---|
| **Micro** | Small, quick | ≤ 30 hours | None — honor system | 100 pts/week |
| **Standard** | Medium, verified | 31–200 hours | Photo, document, or third-party | No limit |
| **Major** | Large, significant | 201+ hours | Institutional proof or formal letter | No limit |

### Micro Track (≤ 30 points)
- **Best for**: Informal, everyday work
- **Examples**: Teaching a child at home, helping a neighbor, small translations
- **Verification**: You just say it happened — we trust you!
- **Weekly limit**: Only 100 points per week to prevent gaming

### Standard Track (31–200 points)
- **Best for**: Organized events, courses, verified activities
- **Examples**: Running an event, teaching a class series, documented projects
- **Verification**: Photo, video, written proof, or a third-party voucher
- **No weekly limit** — earn as much as you can prove

### Major Track (201+ points)
- **Best for**: Significant projects, institutional partnerships
- **Examples**: Building infrastructure, major organizational work
- **Verification**: Official letter, institutional partnership document, formal contract
- **No weekly limit** — these are rare and significant

## How Points Work

When you log a contribution, a **formula** calculates your points based on:

1. **Effort** — How many hours did you spend?
2. **Verification** — How strong is your proof?
3. **Synergy** — Did you team up with people from different skills?
4. **Sustainability** — Is it a one-time effort or ongoing?
5. **Outcome** — Did you achieve measurable results?

The system shows you an **estimated points preview** when you log a contribution, so you know exactly what you'll earn.

### Real Example: Teej Celebration
Organizing a Teej festival is a standard-track contribution (8 hours):

- **Base points**: 8 hours × 10 = 80 points
- **Team bonus**: You worked with dancers, caterers, and marketers (3 different skills) = **1.5× multiplier**
- **Proof quality**: You have photos of the event = **0.7× multiplier** (not full because it's just photos)
- **Sustainability**: One-time event = **1.0× multiplier**
- **Total**: (80 × 1.5 × 0.7) = **84 points**

That's your **public recognition** forever, on your community's records.

## The Contribution Process

```
You Log   →  Your Org  →  Points    →  Your      →  You're on the
Contribution  Reviews It  Awarded    Profile    Leaderboard!
                          Updates    Updated
```

1. **You log it** — Fill in the contribution form with details and optional proof
2. **Status: Pending** — Your contribution is awaiting review
3. **Org reviews** — A community leader verifies it and approves it
4. **Status: Approved** — Points are calculated and added to your account
5. **You see it** — Your points appear on your profile and (optionally) the leaderboard

## What Gets Counted?

✅ **Counts as a contribution:**
- Mentoring (family, friends, community)
- Teaching (classes, workshops, informal)
- Organizing (events, gatherings, meetings)
- Projects (community work, repairs, building)
- Translation / interpretation
- Healthcare / counsel work
- Admin / management for the organisation

❌ **Does NOT count:**
- Paid work (this is only for volunteer/gift work)
- Personal benefit (paid consulting for your own business)
- Harm or illegal activity

## Quick Questions?

- **"Can I delete a contribution?"** — No, contributions are permanent for audit purposes. But you can mark it as "appealed" if there's a mistake.
- **"Who can see my points?"** — That's your choice! Read [Leaderboard and Privacy](05-leaderboard-and-privacy.md).
- **"When do I get my points?"** — After an admin reviews and approves. Usually within a few days.
- **"Can I earn negative points?"** — No. Points can only be zero or positive.
