# Using the Leaderboard for Community Recognition

A guide for organization leaders on how to use the leaderboard to recognize and celebrate volunteers.

---

## Introduction

The leaderboard is **your most powerful tool for recognizing volunteer work.** It shows who's contributing most, makes invisible work visible, and motivates the whole community.

This guide shows how to use it effectively.

---

## Understanding the Leaderboard

### What You See

```
🏆 Contribution Leaderboard

Gold Tier (300+ points)
1. Dr. Sharma              1,250 points  ⭐⭐⭐
2. Mrs. Patel                980 points  ⭐⭐⭐
3. Contributor #5             850 points  ⭐⭐⭐

Silver Tier (150-299 points)
4. Mr. Tamang                720 points  ⭐⭐
5. Contributor #8             650 points  ⭐⭐
6. Raj Sharma                590 points  ⭐⭐
```

### What This Shows

- **Ranking** — Top contributors first
- **Points** — Cumulative lifetime points
- **Tier** — Bronze (50+), Silver (150+), Gold (300+)
- **Privacy** — Some show names, some show "Contributor #X"

## 1. Recognition Ceremonies

The most important use! Public recognition motivates volunteers.

### Monthly Recognition

**Every month**, celebrate your top contributors:

**Example:**

> "This month, Dr. Patel was our top contributor with 150 points from mentoring youth. She spent 15 hours helping 8 young people prepare for university entrance exams. Everyone, let's recognize Dr. Patel!"

**Where to hold it:**
- Monthly community meeting
- Email to all members
- Social media announcement
- WhatsApp group post

**What to recognize:**
- Top 1–3 contributors
- Biggest improvement (person who jumped rankings)
- "Unsung hero" (Anonymous contributor doing great work)

### Annual Awards

**Once per year**, hold a bigger ceremony:

**Gold Tier Award (300+ points)**
- Given to people who earned 300+ lifetime points
- Recognize them by name (or "Contributor #X" if they chose privacy)
- Medal, certificate, or public thank you

**Example:**
> "This year, 5 members reached Gold Tier (300+ points):
> - Dr. Sharma: 1,250 points
> - Mrs. Patel: 980 points
> - Contributor #5: 850 points
> - Raj: 750 points
> - Dr. Patel: 720 points
>
> These five are the backbone of our community. Let's honor them!"

### Recognition Ceremony Ideas

**In-person:**
- Announcement at community gathering
- Medal or certificate given by leader
- Photo with community
- Public thanks

**Virtual:**
- Announcement in email
- Certificate sent by mail
- Recognition video posted
- Spotlight on social media

**Inclusive:**
- Recognize by name if public
- "Contributor #X" if anonymous
- Thank them privately if private
- Everyone sees the appreciation

## 2. Strategic Planning

Use leaderboard data to understand and plan for your organization.

### Identify Core Contributors

**Who are your most active people?**

```
Question: Are you relying too much on a few people?

2026 Leaderboard
1. Dr. Sharma              1,250 points  (30% of all points!)
2. Mrs. Patel                980 points  (24%)
3. Contributor #5             850 points  (21%)
4. Mr. Tamang                720 points  (18%)
5. Others (10 people)         300 points   (7%)

Finding: 3 people doing 75% of work.
Risk: If they burn out, organization suffers.
Action: Develop 5 new leaders. Distribute work.
```

### Identify Talent Gaps

**What skills are we missing?**

```
Question: Are we getting contributions in key areas?

Analysis by Skills
- Teaching/Mentoring: 40% of points ✓
- Event Planning: 30% of points ✓
- Healthcare: 15% of points ✗
- Legal/Admin: 10% of points ✗
- Engineering: 5% of points ✗

Finding: Healthcare and legal skills underrepresented.
Action: Recruit healthcare/legal professionals. Highlight that these skills are valued.
```

### Measure Community Engagement

**Is engagement growing or declining?**

```
Question: Are new people getting involved?

Monthly Trends
January: 15 contributors, 1,200 points earned
February: 18 contributors, 1,400 points earned
March: 21 contributors, 1,600 points earned
April: 22 contributors, 1,750 points earned

Finding: Growing engagement. New people joining.
Action: Sustain this. Keep promoting the system.
```

## 3. Community Building

Use the leaderboard to build community culture.

### Celebrate Diversity of Contribution

**Show that different contributions all matter:**

```
Micro contributors (small, frequent help):
- Aama: 120 points (helping neighbors)
- Ahmed: 100 points (translation)
- Linda: 95 points (mentoring)

Standard contributors (organized activities):
- Raj: 590 points (events, teaching)
- Dr. Patel: 480 points (workshop series)

Major contributors (transformational work):
- Dr. Sharma: 1,250 points (scholarship fund)

Message: "All three tiers are essential. Whether you log 
12 micro-points or 1,250 major-points, you're valued."
```

### Inspire New Contributors

**Show what's possible:**

Show stories of top contributors:
- "How did they get 1,200 points? What did they do?"
- "I could volunteer more if I saw what counts."
- "Wow, this much effort was recognized!"

### Build Friendly Competition

**Motivate through positive competition:**

> "Next month, can we get 3 new Silver Tier members (150+ points)? 
> Last month we had 6, this month we have 8. Let's keep growing!"

**Not:** "Trying to beat someone else"  
**Yes:** "Trying to beat last year's numbers"

## Monthly Recognition Process

### Step 1: Check the Leaderboard (5 min)

```
Top 5 for April:
1. Dr. Sharma: 1,250 points
2. Mrs. Patel: 980 points
3. Contributor #5: 850 points
4. Mr. Tamang: 720 points
5. Raj: 650 points

New to top 5: Contributor #5 (jumped from #8)
```

### Step 2: Prepare Recognition (15 min)

**For public members:**
- Name: Dr. Sharma
- Points: 1,250
- What they did: "Established a scholarship fund. 25 students now funded. On track to serve 100+ over 10 years."

**For anonymous contributors:**
- Rank: #5
- Points: 850
- What they did: "Led organizing, marketing, and translation for Teej festival. 150+ attendees."

### Step 3: Announce (5–10 min)

**Announcement script:**

> "Thank you all for your volunteer work this month. Let's recognize our top contributors.
>
> In 3rd place with 850 points: Contributor #5. They coordinated skills in event planning, marketing, and translation. They led our Teej festival with 150 attendees. Thank you!
>
> In 2nd place with 980 points: Mrs. Patel. She ran our weekly health workshop, reached 50+ seniors, and created lasting health education for our community. We're grateful!
>
> In 1st place with 1,250 points: Dr. Sharma. She established our scholarship fund, which has already changed 25 lives and will serve 100+ students long-term. Her vision and effort are transformational.
>
> Everyone, please join me in thanking these volunteers!"

### Step 4: Celebrate (varies)

- Award certificate or medal
- Send personal thank-you note
- Post on social media
- Share story with broader community

## Annual Awards Ceremony

### Format

**Full ceremony (1–2 hours):**

1. **Welcome** — Explain the contribution system (10 min)
2. **Data overview** — Total points, growth, impact (10 min)
3. **Awards** — Gold/Silver/Bronze tier recognition (20 min)
4. **Stories** — Why each person's work matters (20 min)
5. **Celebration** — Food, music, socializing (30+ min)

### Recognition Tiers

**Gold Tier (300+ points):**
- Highest honor
- Public announcement
- Certificate + medal
- Photo with community
- Feature in newsletter

**Silver Tier (150–299 points):**
- Recognition at ceremony
- Certificate
- Public thanks
- Optional: Feature in newsletter

**Bronze Tier (50–149 points):**
- Certificate or badge
- Announced at ceremony
- Thank you email

### Example Ceremony Speech

> "Friends, today we celebrate everyone who made our community stronger this year.
>
> In total, 45 volunteers contributed 18,500 points of work. That's equivalent to 1,850 hours of community service. By market rate, that's $50,000+ of donated work.
>
> But more importantly, this work changed lives.
>
> Dr. Sharma's scholarship fund now supports 25 students. Mrs. Patel's health workshops reached 300+ seniors. Raj's mentoring helped 8 youth prepare for university. And 40 other members served their neighbors in countless ways.
>
> Today, we honor the Gold Tier members who each contributed 300+ points:
> [Names or Contributor #X]
>
> We honor the Silver Tier members:
> [Names]
>
> We honor all Bronze Tier members and those who chose privacy.
>
> You are the reason this community thrives. Thank you."

## Using the Leaderboard for Outreach

### Recruit New Volunteers

**Show people what's possible:**

> "See the leaderboard? Raj earned 650 points by organizing events, teaching, and mentoring. You could do the same! Help us serve our community."

### Highlight Skill Areas

**Show where you need help:**

> "We're looking for people with healthcare, legal, or engineering skills. These are valued! Check the leaderboard — see how engineers/doctors/lawyers contribute. We need more of you!"

### Celebrate Small Contributions

**Show that micro-track matters:**

> "Don't have 40 hours? Micro contributions count too! Aama earns points just by teaching her grandchildren Nepali. Ahmed earns points translating documents. Both are celebrated. Join us."

---

## Cautions

### Don't Compare People

❌ "Why does Raj have more points than Ahmed?"  
✅ "Both Raj and Ahmed are valued. They contribute in different ways."

### Don't Shame Private Contributors

❌ "Why aren't you on the leaderboard? Are you lazy?"  
✅ "Thank you for contributing. You chose privacy, and we respect that."

### Don't Game the System

❌ Award points for not-real contributions  
✓ Ensure admins properly verify contributions before awarding

---

## Questions?

- **"Should I tell people about leaderboard before they contribute?"** — Yes! It motivates. Explain that recognition is optional.
- **"What if someone feels left out?"** — Celebrate all contributions, not just top 5. Recognition should be inclusive.
- **"How often should I run ceremonies?"** — Monthly recognition + Annual awards works well.
- **"What if no one's interested in being recognized?"** — That's okay. Still celebrate publicly. Some people contribute quietly.
